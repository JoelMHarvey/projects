How it works

Three steps to a flock

An old iPhone or iPad by the cage becomes your bird's window to a friend. You stay in control from your own phone.

1

Set up the perch

Mount a spare iPhone or iPad near the cage and pair it with a one-time 6-digit code. It becomes your bird's dedicated companion terminal — always plugged in, always ready.

2

Find a feathered friend

Create your bird's profile and browse matches by species and schedule overlap. Both owners must approve before any session can ever happen.

3

Let them chatter

Sessions start on schedule or with one tap from your phone. Two birds see and hear each other over live, low-latency video — and you can peek in any time.

Trust & safety

Built cage-first, privacy-first

A camera in your home should earn its place. Here's how LonelyBirds does.

No video ever stored

Streams flow peer-to-peer between the two terminals and vanish the moment a session ends. No recordings, no clips, no server-side media — by architecture, not policy.

Mutual consent, always

Both owners must approve a connection before a single frame is exchanged. No approval, no session — by any means.

You set the hours

Sessions only run inside the availability windows you define, and end automatically at the boundary or a max duration. The terminal never runs indefinitely.

End it instantly

Either owner can stop a live session or pause, block, or delete a connection at any moment, from anywhere. Your home, your rules.

Report & review

Flag any connection with one tap. Flagged connections are suspended immediately, pending human review.

Live view for owners only

You can silently peek at your own bird's live session to check everything's fine. Nobody outside the two approved households can ever watch.
