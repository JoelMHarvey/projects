# A FRESH START
### A Black Mirror Episode
*Written for screen*
*Based on the novel A Fresh Start*

---

## CAST

**MEI** — Late twenties. English teacher. A face that is open in the way that some faces are open — not naive, just genuinely present.

**WEI** — Early thirties. Infrastructure logistics. Patient, precise, forward-looking. Loves his wife in a practical, walls-that-hold way.

**DANIEL** — British, late thirties. Works for an American bank. A face that people read as uninvested. He is always listening.

**KENJI** — Japanese, forty. Architect. An architect's eyes — they read the structural logic of things before the emotional content.

**LIN** — Eight years old. Unusually still. Watches rather than speaks.

**THE TEACHER** — Late twenties. Lin, grown. Same quality of attention as her mother. It costs something to maintain.

---

## NOTE ON LANGUAGE

All dialogue between Daniel and Mei is in English — the bridge language, neither of theirs, on soil that belonged to neither of them.

Daniel and Kenji speak English together for the audience, with occasional Japanese (subtitled).

Mei and Wei speak Mandarin throughout (subtitled).

The language gap is always present. It is the point.

## COLD OPEN

**INT. CLASSROOM — DONGFANG SHENG PROVINCE — DAY**

```
Bright. Clean. Thirty small desks in neat rows. A wall 
display shows a map: EASTERN DEVELOPMENT ZONES, annotated 
in Mandarin. On another wall, a class photograph — forty 
children, Chinese faces, mountain backdrop.

A TEACHER stands at the front. Late twenties. A quality 
of attention about her, the kind that costs something 
to maintain. She holds a textbook open.

Students write in notebooks. Ordinary Tuesday.
```

**TEACHER (MANDARIN)**
*(reading)*
"The Eastern Development Zones represent one of the
most ambitious infrastructure programmes of the early
twenty-first century. Established following the Pacific
Territorial Resolution of 2025..."

```
A GIRL — eight years old, neat uniform, unusually still 
— watches the teacher rather than writing.
```

**TEACHER (MANDARIN)**
"...the province is home to an estimated 3.2 million
residents, the majority of whom relocated under the
Pioneer Family Programme beginning in 2025..."

```
The girl raises her hand.

The teacher looks up.
```

**GIRL (MANDARIN)**
What was here before?

```
A beat. Something crosses the teacher's face — too fast 
to read.
```

**TEACHER (MANDARIN)**
Mountains. And the Pacific Ocean.

*(pause)*

Next question.

```
The girl doesn't write this down. She keeps watching 
the teacher.

BELL. Chairs scrape. Children file out, talking, 
laughing, ordinary. The girl stays behind. She reaches 
into her bag and places something on the teacher's 
desk.

A small ceramic dish. Old. A crack runs across its 
face, filled with gold lacquer — the repair more 
visible than the damage, gleaming under the 
fluorescent light.

The girl is already moving toward the door.
```

**TEACHER (MANDARIN)**
Where did you get this?

**GIRL (MANDARIN)**
My mother said to give it to you.

*(at the door)*

She said you'd know what it was.

```
The girl leaves.

The teacher stands alone with the dish on the empty 
desk. She doesn't move for a moment.

Then she picks it up. Turns it. The gold seam catches 
the light — the crack honoured rather than hidden, 
beautiful in a way she doesn't have a word for.

She looks at it for a long time.

She looks at the mountain through the window.

She sets the dish down very carefully.

She sits.
```

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**The technology:** The AI invasion force is never shown directly. We hear drones. We see bootprints. We see the machine in the cemetery. We see road signs that update. The horror is systemic — the infrastructure of erasure, not any individual act of cruelty.

**The kintsugi dish:** Establish in the cottage early (Kenji finding it in the cupboard). It should appear in at least four shots before the gate scene: on the shelf, Kenji drawing it, Daniel wrapping it, the gate. Pay off in the cold open/return — the dish older, worn smooth from decades of handling.

**The shrine:** Mei tends it throughout. It should be in every outdoor sequence she appears in, present or in frame. By the final autumn sequence the audience should feel its absence or presence before Mei reaches the bend.

**Lin writing on the board:** The four Japanese syllables she writes are never shown to camera. We see her hand move. We see the characters from a distance — legible to anyone who reads Japanese, illegible to the dominant culture of the classroom. The audience who can read it knows. The audience who can't experiences exactly what the story is about.

**Sound design:** The carrier wave from the radio should return as a motif — briefly, in the cold open, before the title card, and again in the final black. A flat tone. Steady. Transmitting nothing.
